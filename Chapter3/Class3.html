Family Impersonation

The risk: A stranger poses as a relative in distress to obtain an urgent wire transfer.

  • How it works: The fraudster retrieves first names or locations from your social media to appear credible. They often use a new phone number, claiming the old one was lost or stolen to justify why you don't recognize the contact.

  • Example: A young man calls you: "Hello Grandma? It’s me, I’ve had an accident and my phone is broken, I’m calling you from a friend's phone". He explains that he needs to pay for a tow truck immediately and asks you for an urgent transfer to his "friend's" account to help him out.

What you must absolutely not do:

  • Never give money without having spoken directly to the person on their usual number.

  • Do not share family life details or vacation dates on internet profiles visible to everyone.

  • Do not give in to panic if the person asks you to "not say anything to the parents".

Be careful: a similar email or one using the name of a relative may be used.

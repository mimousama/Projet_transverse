Theft via Text Message (Smishing)

The risk: Being deceived by a short message (SMS) received on your mobile phone.

  • How it works: The hacker uses computer tools to mask their number and display a known name, such as "Netflix", "Postal Service", or "ANTAI". The goal is to make you leave the secure environment of your phone to lure you to a web page that they control.

  • Example: An SMS arrives: "Info: you have an unpaid fine of €35, settle before the penalty on this site..." [cite : 215]. Driven by worry, you click and arrive at a site that looks exactly like the official government website. By paying the fine, you are actually giving your banking "key" to the thief.

What you must absolutely not do:

  • Do not respond to SMS messages requesting urgent action or payment.

  • Do not call back unknown numbers that leave you vague messages asking you to contact them immediately.

  • Do not click on any link received via SMS. Check if they are trustworthy with a free link checker on Internet or by calling the company directly with a known number.

Be careful: a similar email or one using the name of a relative may also be used.
